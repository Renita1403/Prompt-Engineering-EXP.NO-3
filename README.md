Example 2:

Prompt:what is greenhouse effect?what are its causes?
The greenhouse effect is the process by which certain gases in the Earth’s atmosphere trap heat from the Sun, keeping the planet warm enough to support life. Without this natural effect, Earth would be too cold to live on.
Causes of the Greenhouse Effect:
The greenhouse effect is mainly caused by the increase of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, such as:
Carbon dioxide (CO₂) – Released from burning fossil fuels like coal, petrol, and diesel.
a)	Methane (CH₄) – Produced from livestock, rice fields, and landfills.
b)	Nitrous oxide (N₂O) – Emitted from fertilizers and industrial activities.
c)	Deforestation – Cutting down trees reduces the absorption of CO₂.
d)	Industrial pollution – Factories and vehicles release harmful gases into the air.
e)	When these gases increase, more heat gets trapped in the atmosphere, leading to global warming.
